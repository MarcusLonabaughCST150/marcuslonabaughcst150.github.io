Rachel Sandwich

What constitutes a Rueben sandwich versus a Rachel sandwich is a hotly debated subject that varies wildly depending on where you grew up and who you grew up with. For now, lets assume that a Rueben has sourkraut and a Rachel has coleslaw, but you can make these sandwiches any way you like. Varying the meats, breads and sauces are what make cooking sandwiches interesting. This uses my coleslaw recipe that you can find here, and corned beef which you can find instructions on here.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of corned beef
  • 2 slices of bread
  • 1/4 cup of cole slaw
  • 1 slice of cheese


How to cook

  1. Place the cooked corned beef in a pan on medium-low heat and reheat for 1 minute on each side.
  2. Butter one side of each piece of bread.
  3. Place a piece of bread butter side down on the pan, then place the cheese on it followed by the meat.
  4. Place the other piece of bread butter side up on top and let heat for 2 minutes.
  5. Carefully but quickly flip the sandwich and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. After the cheese is melted and the bread is toasted, place the sandwich on a plate.
  7. Once it is cool enough to touch, lift the piece of bread without melted cheese up and place the cole slaw on top.
  8. Cut in half and serve!
